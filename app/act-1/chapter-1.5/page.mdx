# Chapter 1.5: Hardware Operations

<ChapterCard>
### The Briefing: Binary Arithmetic
The Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) performs all calculations using binary addition as its core operation.
* **Addition:** Follows the rules $0+0=0$, $0+1=1$, $1+0=1$, and $1+1=0$ (with carry 1).
* **Subtraction:** $A - B$ is executed as $A + (\text{2's complement of } B)$.
* **Multiplication:** Achieved through Shift and Add operations. Multiplying by $2^n$ is a Left Shift by $n$ bits.

The Ripple Carry Adder (RCA) is the most basic implementation, where the carry out of the $i$-th full adder becomes the carry in for the $(i+1)$-th full adder.
</ChapterCard>

<GateTrap>
**Trap: Propagation Delay Accumulation**
In an $n$-bit Ripple Carry Adder, the Most Significant Bit cannot produce its final correct sum until the carry has rippled through all $n-1$ previous stages. The total delay is proportional to $O(n)$. Do not calculate the delay of an RCA as simply the delay of a single full adder. A single full adder has a delay of $2$ gate levels for the Carry, so an $n$-bit RCA takes $2n$ gate delays to stabilize.
</GateTrap>

<ArchitectShortcut>
**Shortcut: Fast Binary Multiplication by Powers of 2**
If asked to multiply a binary number by a decimal value like $10$, do not use long multiplication. Decompose it into powers of 2: $10 = 8 + 2$.
So, $X \times 10 = X \times (2^3) + X \times (2^1)$.
Shift $X$ left by 3 bits, shift $X$ left by 1 bit, and add the two results.
</ArchitectShortcut>
