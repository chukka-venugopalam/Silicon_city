# Chapter 1.6: Boolean Laws and Identities

<ChapterCard>
### The Briefing: Digital Optimization
The cost of a digital circuit is directly proportional to the number of logic gates. Boolean Algebra provides the mathematical framework to simplify expressions before hardware implementation.
Key Laws:
* **Idempotent Law:** $A + A = A$, $A \cdot A = A$
* **Absorption Law:** $A + AB = A$, $A(A + B) = A$
* **De Morgan's Laws:** $(A + B)' = A' \cdot B'$ and $(AB)' = A' + B'$
* **Consensus Theorem:** $AB + A'C + BC = AB + A'C$. The term $BC$ is the "consensus" term and is entirely redundant.
</ChapterCard>

<GateTrap>
**Trap: False Distribution**
A fatal error in Boolean simplification is assuming $(A+B)(C+D) = AC + BD$. You must foil it out completely: $AC + AD + BC + BD$.
Similarly, do not forget the dual of distribution: $A + BC = (A+B)(A+C)$. This identity is frequently used to simplify complex POS expressions but is highly unintuitive to students used to standard algebra.
</GateTrap>

<ArchitectShortcut>
**Shortcut: Fast Consensus Identification**
To immediately spot the Consensus Theorem in an exam question, look for THREE terms containing THREE variables (e.g., $A, B, C$). 
1. Find the variable that is normal in one term and complemented in another (e.g., $A$ in $AB$, $A'$ in $A'C$).
2. The third term MUST be the product of the remaining variables from the first two terms ($B$ and $C$).
If it matches, instantly cross out the third term.
</ArchitectShortcut>
